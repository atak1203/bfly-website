# SkyWipe Autopilot Core — BFL'Y SUAS
import skywipe as sw
from perception import SLAMEngine, DirtDetector

class MissionController:
  def __init__(self, config):
    self.slam  = SLAMEngine(res="high")
    self.vision = DirtDetector(thresh=0.82)
    self.path  = CoveragePathPlanner()

  async def run_mission(self, facade_map):
    plan = await self.path.compute(facade_map)
    for waypoint in plan:
      await sw.fly_to(waypoint)
      dirty = await self.vision.check()
      if dirty: await sw.clean()

Details

A closer look.

The monocoque carbon-fiber chassis weighs only 420 g while achieving a torsional rigidity that absorbs vibration from the cleaning module, protecting both optics and mechanical components.

Four 2306 2450KV motors are individually controlled by 35A ESCs with active braking, enabling sub-centimeter positioning accuracy even in 5 m/s crosswinds near high-rise facades.

A 16-beam rotating LiDAR generates 300,000 points per second. Combined with stereo cameras, the system builds a millimeter-accurate map of any glass surface before the first brush touch.

An 800 ml pressurized reservoir feeds six precision nozzles. A moisture sensor in the brush head dynamically adjusts flow rate, eliminating streaks and reducing water use by 80% vs rope-access teams.

A 6S 5000 mAh LiPo hot-swap system means zero downtime. While one battery pack flies, another charges at the ground station — enabling back-to-back missions without interruption.

General Info

Baykar Fen Lisesi SUAS SKY
WIPE

SkyWipe is the inaugural vehicle of the BFL'Y SUAS team — a fully autonomous unmanned aerial system designed to clean glass surfaces on high-rise buildings without any rope-access infrastructure. Built ground-up by students of Baykar Science High School, it represents the convergence of aerospace engineering, computer vision, and mechatronics in a single 1.4 kg platform.

The vehicle integrates a custom-developed autopilot stack with a proprietary cleaning module, enabling it to plan, execute, and verify complete facade cleaning missions. Its modular architecture allows rapid reconfiguration for different building profiles, surface materials, and weather conditions — setting the foundation for a scalable urban cleaning system.
